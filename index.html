Built for design lessons

The editor keeps the voxel-building strength of the original project, but the framing is classroom-safe and process-led.

Prototype spaces

Place terrain, paths, buildings, planting, water, and details to make ideas visible quickly.

Shape constraints

Raise, lower, paint, and tune terrain so students can test access, flow, elevation, and environmental choices.

Review from viewpoints

Switch cameras and inspect the design from above, at ground level, and through perspective views.

Export evidence

Save locally, duplicate versions, copy share links, and use screenshots to explain iteration.

Classroom focus

Use it as a design-process tool, not a game economy.

Brief

Start with a problem, audience, constraints, success criteria, and evidence students must produce.

Prototype

Build fast versions, label design decisions verbally or in a worksheet, then duplicate for alternatives.

Critique

Review against criteria: access, user needs, sustainability, clarity, circulation, scale, and trade-offs.

Iterate

Improve the design and capture before/after evidence. The point is reasoning, not decoration.

Brief. Prototype. Critique. Iterate.

A lesson loop that makes design choices visible.

  1. 1

    Define the brief

    Who is the space for? What constraints matter? What will count as success?

  2. 2

    Build a first prototype

    Block out the idea quickly. Keep it rough enough that students still want to change it.

  3. 3

    Review and improve

    Use peer critique and screenshots to justify revisions with evidence.
